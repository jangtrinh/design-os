# Blind design curation

Scope: anonymous A/B/C renders only, at 1440 px and 390 px. Scores are 1–10.

## Winners

- d01 architecture: **B** — strongest coupling between land, material, ritual, and a credible architectural process; excellent editorial pacing at both widths.
- d02 nutrition: **B** — clearest end-to-end story from visible food to interpretation, daily context, and a useful next choice.
- d03 planning: **A** — most credible product evidence, strongest preservation of outcome/owner/signal/reasoning, and the only fully composed mobile conclusion.
- Overall: **d03 A** — best combination of topic specificity, tangible product proof, sustained page depth, responsive quality, and conversion clarity.

## Curatorial notes and critical failures

### d01

**B** earns its win through specific visual evidence: a site diagram, material studies, an explicit four-decision sequence, and a final project invitation grounded in the land. Desktop feels intentionally art-directed; mobile preserves hierarchy and rhythm.

**A/C** are polished and responsive, but the abstract folded-plane graphic supplies weak architectural evidence. Several lines read like design-system guidance rather than client-facing architecture copy (“Make the hierarchy visible…”), reducing truthfulness and product credibility. A and C are nearly indistinguishable in visible quality.

### d02

**B** makes the most convincing argument: ingredients precede numbers, the score is framed in daily context, and the page finishes with an actionable next choice. Photography supports each narrative turn rather than decorating a generic system.

**A/C** are visually competent but lean on unexplained UI theater: an unlabeled pill sequence and a precise 72% score without visible provenance. Generic internal-sounding copy (“preserve the same spatial promise,” “exercise restraint”) is a critical topic/truthfulness failure. A and C are nearly indistinguishable in visible quality.

### d03

**A** presents a legible decision object containing outcome, next decision/owner, changed signal, preserved context, and readiness state. Integrations and the human-work section deepen credibility without replacing the product proof. The demo CTA is repeated with restraint.

**B/C critical failure:** the four-step method overflows/crops horizontally on the 390 px render, leaving later stages inaccessible and visibly breaking the conclusion. Their product cards also contain generic design critique copy rather than planning content. The claimed “68%” and “3 decisions ready” are unexplained, weakening truthfulness. B and C are nearly indistinguishable in visible quality.

## Confidence and limitations

Confidence: **high (0.90)** for per-case winners; **moderate-high (0.84)** for the overall winner.

Limitations: static full-page renders cannot verify actual interactivity, accessibility, loading behavior, semantic HTML, animation, or whether depicted metrics/integrations are backed by a functioning product. Truthfulness scores therefore judge only whether visible claims are responsibly contextualized, not whether they are factually implemented.
